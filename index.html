ABOUT US

History

BMW's origins trace back to the early 20th century. Initially, it started as an aircraft engine manufacturer during World War I. After the war, BMW shifted to making motorcycles in 1923, followed by automobiles in 1928.

Our Global Presence

Global Presence: BMW operates in more than 150 countries, with manufacturing plants in Germany, the U.S., China, South Africa, and other locations.

Philosophy

BMW’s philosophy, encapsulated in the tagline "The Ultimate Driving Machine," is deeply rooted in the company’s commitment to delivering exceptional performance, precision engineering, and an unparalleled driving experience.

MEMBERS

Susanne klatten

Susanne Klatten owns about 19% of automaker BMW; her brother, Stefan Quandt, owns nearly 24%.

Stefan Quandt

deputy chairman of BMW and owns around 25.6% of the company

Franz Josef

Franz Josef Popp was one of three men responsible for the founding of BMW AG and the First General Director of BMW AG from 1922 to 1942.

Camillo Castiglioni

Camillo Castiglioni was a financier and aircraft construction pioneer who played a major role in the development of BMW.

Gustav Otto

In 1916, Gustav Otto founded the Bayerische Flugzeugwerke AG (Bavarian Aircraft Works), which later became BMW.

Karl Rapp

Karl Friedrich Rapp (1882 - 1962) was a German mechanical engineer and the founder of Rapp Motorenwerke, which later became BMW.
